## What the official source establishes

- Sponsored Products can use automatic targeting, where Amazon matches ads to relevant shopping queries and products, and manual targeting, where sellers choose keywords or products.
- Automatic targeting is useful for discovery and for identifying shopper queries that convert.
- Manual keyword targeting gives more control over keywords and match types.
- Product targeting can target specific products, categories, brands, or product attributes where supported.
- Targeting choices should be reviewed with reports rather than treated as one-time setup.

## Operational interpretation for Amazon sellers

- For launch-stage ASINs, keep automatic and broad discovery alive while isolating obviously irrelevant traffic.
- Harvest converting or high-intent search terms into controlled manual campaigns.
- Use product targeting for competitor, complementary product, and category-shelf strategies when offer and listing are competitive.
- Separate discovery and efficiency campaigns so budget and bid changes do not erase learning.

## Evidence required before action

- Campaign structure, targeting type, search term report, targeting report, advertised product report.
- ASIN launch stage, budget ceiling, margin and target ACoS, protected discovery terms.
- Offer competitiveness: price, coupon, rating, review count, inventory and Featured Offer status.

## Guardrails

- Do not shut down automatic discovery only because early traffic is inefficient; first separate irrelevant from relevant-but-not-yet-converting traffic.
- Do not harvest every click term; require conversion, high intent, or strategic relevance.
- Do not expand product targeting if offer, reviews, price, or inventory cannot support conversion.
