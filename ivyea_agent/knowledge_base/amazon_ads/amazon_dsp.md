# Amazon DSP buying and measurement boundaries

Source basis: Amazon Ads official Amazon DSP product page. Availability, inventory, service models, fees, and minimums vary by region and contract.

## Product boundary

- Amazon DSP is an omnichannel demand-side platform for programmatic media buying across Amazon and eligible third-party supply.
- It supports multiple formats and deal types, including auction and negotiated/programmatic inventory, subject to account access and region.
- Advertisers do not necessarily need to sell products on Amazon to use Amazon DSP.
- Self-service and managed-service access have different operating and commercial arrangements.

## Required campaign context

- advertiser, region, order/line item, objective, supply source, deal type, format, device, audience, bid/fee model, and frequency controls;
- creative approval and destination/conversion configuration;
- reach, frequency, viewability, spend, conversions, new-to-brand or other proprietary metrics with definitions;
- attribution source, lookback, pixel/event implementation, and any third-party measurement.

## Guardrails

- Do not compare DSP spend or ROAS with sponsored ads before accounting for supply, fees, view-through attribution, objective, and conversion scope.
- Audience membership and modeled signals are targeting inputs, not statements about an identifiable individual's behavior.
- Published service minimums and availability are indicative only; verify the current local contract and account access.
