# Sponsored Brands product and optimization boundaries

Source basis: Amazon Ads official Sponsored Brands guide. Formats, eligibility, placements, billing options, and moderation requirements change over time.

## Product boundary

- Sponsored Brands supports brand-focused creative such as video, image, and product collections, with destinations that can include Brand Stores or detail pages.
- Campaign targeting can use keyword or product/category approaches, subject to the selected format and current console/API capabilities.
- Eligibility commonly depends on advertiser type and Brand Registry status; verify the target account and marketplace.
- Creative is moderated and must match the landing page, advertised products, language, trademark permissions, and current advertising policies.

## Required measurement context

- campaign objective, format, destination, targeting type, placement, bidding/billing model, marketplace, and eligibility state;
- creative version and moderation status;
- impressions, clicks, spend, attributed orders/sales, new-to-brand metrics where available, and the exact attribution definition;
- inventory and retail-readiness state of promoted products and destination pages.

## Guardrails

- Do not compare Sponsored Brands directly with Sponsored Products without aligning objective, placement, attribution, sales scope, and funnel stage.
- Brand discovery or new-to-brand metrics do not by themselves prove incrementality.
- Treat recommended keyword counts, budgets, and review times as guidance, not universal guarantees.
