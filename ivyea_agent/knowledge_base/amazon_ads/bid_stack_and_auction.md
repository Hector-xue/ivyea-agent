# Sponsored Products Bid Stack And Auction Boundaries

Updated: 2026-07-06

## Official facts

- Sponsored Products is a cost-per-click product: advertisers set budgets and bids and are charged for eligible clicks.
- Dynamic bidding can lower or raise a bid based on Amazon's assessment of conversion likelihood. The current guide states that dynamic up-and-down can change bids by up to 100% for placements.
- Placement adjustments can apply to top of search, rest of search, product pages, and Amazon Business where available.
- A placement adjustment and dynamic bidding can stack. The configured base bid is therefore not always the maximum effective auction bid.
- Current placement options and stacking behavior are time-sensitive product capabilities and must be rechecked before automated bulk actions.

## Analysis model

Record base bid, bidding strategy, placement adjustments, budget state, targeting type, marketplace, change time, and report maturity. Explain the configured stack before attributing a result to a single bid input.

## Auction boundary

- A higher eligible bid does not guarantee impressions, a placement, clicks, sales, or organic rank.
- Public documentation does not expose a complete auction or organic-ranking formula.
- Account performance after a bid change is an account observation. A repeatable test may support an operating inference, not a universal Amazon weighting rule.
