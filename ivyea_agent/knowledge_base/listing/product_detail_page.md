## Core Facts

- A product listing has a product detail page and one or more seller offers.
- A product detail page includes product name, images, description, bullet points, product details, offer information, and safety/compliance information where applicable.
- Product description gives a broad overview of the product and its benefits.
- Bullet points can highlight important details and attributes.
- Sellers can upload up to nine product photos.
- Clear, accurate, high-resolution images on a simple backdrop help customers understand the product.
- Search terms and keywords can be used in product titles, descriptions, bullet points, and other listing fields to help customers find products.
- Brand Analytics can provide search query and top search term insight for enrolled brands.
- Product reviews and ratings can influence customer purchase decisions.
- A+ Content can add enhanced images, videos, custom text, and comparison modules for eligible brands.
- Manage Your Experiments can test listing content variants for eligible products and brands.

## Ivyea Agent Rules

- Treat winning advertising search terms as listing coverage candidates, not just ad targets.
- Do not force every winner term into the title. Decide placement by intent and readability:
  - title: primary product identity and highest-value exact relevance;
  - bullets: attributes, use cases, compatibility, dimensions, material, benefits;
  - images/A+: visual proof, comparison, scenarios, objections;
  - backend/search terms: supporting variants that should not clutter customer-facing copy.
- If ad clicks are high but conversion is weak for a relevant term, diagnose listing, price, review count/rating, offer, image clarity, and mismatch before negating.
- If a term is a replacement/accessory/compatibility query, verify the product actually satisfies that intent before adding it to listing copy.
- Avoid claims that cannot be supported by product evidence or compliance information.
- Listing recommendations should separate evidence from inference.

## Listing Gap Checklist

- Does the listing clearly say what the product is?
- Does it cover the strongest converting search terms?
- Does it cover use cases and scenarios found in search terms?
- Does it cover compatibility, size, material, quantity, and key constraints?
- Do images answer the main conversion objections?
- Are review/rating and price competitive enough for the traffic being bought?
- Is A+ used to explain differences, proof points, and brand story?
- Should a proposed wording change be tested through Manage Your Experiments?

## Ad-to-Listing Diagnosis

- Winner term missing from listing: consider adding to bullet/A+/image copy.
- High clicks, no orders, relevant term: inspect image, title promise, price, review, and feature mismatch.
- High clicks, no orders, irrelevant term: consider negative exact/phrase.
- Competitor/ASIN term: treat as conquest or product-targeting decision, not automatic negative.
