# GTIN, product identity, and exemption diagnostics

Source basis: Amazon's public GTIN guide and authenticated Product ID/GTIN help. Marketplace, category, brand, and current workflow requirements can differ.

## Official facts

- Amazon identifies UPC, ISBN, EAN, and JAN as common GTIN/product-ID forms used for product identification.
- A GTIN can be used to match an offer to an existing catalog product or to create a new product listing. ASIN, SKU, and GTIN serve different purposes and are not interchangeable.
- A seller that creates/owns a product can obtain a GTIN through GS1; a reseller should normally obtain the correct product identifier from the manufacturer/source.
- Amazon's public guide says some products may be eligible for a GTIN exemption when no matching catalog product exists. Eligibility and required evidence must be taken from the live listing workflow and current help page.

## Diagnostic intake

- marketplace, product type/category, brand value/casing, title, packaging images, manufacturer, and whether the product already exists in the catalog;
- submitted product ID and type, exact error code/message, and current Product Type Definitions requirements;
- exemption application status and exact approved product type/brand, if applicable;
- variation structure and product ID for every distinct child product.

## Guardrails

- Do not buy or invent a random UPC, reuse another product's GTIN, or create a duplicate detail page for an identical product.
- Do not assume a GTIN exemption applies to a different brand spelling, product type, marketplace, or variation child.
- Do not treat exemption approval as exemption from accurate brand/product identity or other listing requirements.
