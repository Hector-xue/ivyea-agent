## What the official source establishes

- Fulfillment by Amazon is Amazon’s fulfillment service where sellers send inventory to Amazon and Amazon handles storage, picking, packing, shipping, customer service, and returns for eligible orders.
- FBA can support Prime eligibility and delivery promises where requirements are met.
- FBA has operational requirements, fees, inventory processes, and compliance obligations.
- Inventory availability and fulfillment quality affect whether advertising can convert and scale reliably.

## Operational interpretation for Amazon sellers

- Before increasing ad budget or bids, confirm inventory days and fulfillment readiness.
- For launch ASINs, avoid aggressive scaling if inbound inventory or replenishment is uncertain.
- When conversion drops, check stock status, delivery promise, and offer eligibility before blaming traffic quality.
- FBA fee structure and storage constraints must be considered in target ACoS and margin calculations.

## Evidence required before action

- Inventory days, inbound shipments, available units, reserved units, stranded inventory, FBA fees, price, margin.
- Buy Box/Featured Offer status, delivery promise, Prime eligibility, account/inventory alerts.
- Ad spend and sales trend before and after inventory or fulfillment changes.

## Guardrails

- Do not scale ads into a likely stockout unless the strategy explicitly accepts rank/availability risk.
- Do not interpret low conversion as keyword waste when offer delivery promise or inventory is weak.
- Do not ignore FBA fees when setting target ACoS.
