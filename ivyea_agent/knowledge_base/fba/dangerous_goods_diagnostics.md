# FBA dangerous goods and hazmat diagnostics

Source basis: authenticated Seller Central dangerous-goods guidance plus Amazon's public dangerous-goods shipping announcement. This card does not determine a product's legal classification.

## Official boundary

Amazon describes dangerous goods as products that can create risk during storage, handling, or transport because of characteristics such as flammability, pressure, corrosion, or other harmful substances. Amazon gathers product information to determine whether and how a product can be fulfilled.

Common examples can include certain products containing lithium-ion batteries, aerosols, alcohol, adhesives, cosmetics, or chemicals, but classification must use the actual formulation, battery, packaging, quantity, transport mode, marketplace, and current Amazon request.

## Evidence required

- exact ASIN/SKU and marketplace;
- product formulation/materials, battery chemistry/configuration, net quantity, packaging, and intended use;
- exact dangerous-goods review/status message;
- current Safety Data Sheet or exemption sheet when requested, including product/brand/manufacturer match, issue/revision date, and complete sections;
- prior submissions and the specific rejection reason;
- fulfillment/shipping program and destination restrictions.

## Diagnostic workflow

1. Identify whether the problem is missing attributes, document quality/mismatch, Amazon review status, FBA eligibility, storage limit, or carrier/legal restriction.
2. Match every submitted document to the exact product and current marketplace request.
3. Preserve the review message and document version; do not infer acceptance from upload success alone.
4. Recheck the authenticated current help page because program eligibility and document requirements can change.

## Guardrails

- Do not classify a product as non-dangerous from marketing copy or an old supplier statement.
- Do not edit an SDS or exemption sheet to force acceptance.
- Do not advise shipping through an ineligible program or concealing batteries/chemicals.
