## What the official source establishes

- Amazon seller education emphasizes inventory management as a core operating discipline for avoiding stockouts, excess inventory, and fulfillment problems.
- Inventory planning connects demand forecasting, replenishment timing, storage constraints, and sales velocity.
- Advertising can accelerate sell-through, so inventory must be checked before scaling campaigns.
- Poor inventory health can harm sales momentum and customer experience.

## Operational interpretation for Amazon sellers

- Use inventory days as a gate before budget or bid increases.
- If inventory is constrained, prioritize efficient exact winners and reduce wasteful exploration rather than broadly scaling traffic.
- If excess inventory exists and margin allows, advertising and promotions can support sell-through, but conversion readiness must still be checked.
- Link weekly ad review with replenishment planning.

## Evidence required before action

- Available inventory, inbound dates, sales velocity, forecast, storage/aged inventory risk, margin, promotion plan.
- Campaign spend/sales, TACoS, budget caps, target ACoS, seasonality.
- Listing and offer readiness before using ads as a liquidation lever.

## Guardrails

- Do not increase traffic without confirming inventory can fulfill demand.
- Do not pause all discovery solely due to low inventory if future replenishment and rank strategy require controlled continuity.
- Do not use ads to force sell-through when conversion issues or policy/listing problems are unresolved.
