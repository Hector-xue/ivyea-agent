# Amazon Japan consumption tax (JCT) and qualified-invoice boundaries

Source basis: Amazon Japan's Seller Central guidance on the Japanese Consumption Tax (JCT) qualified-invoice (適格請求書/インボイス) system and the authenticated tax settings. Rules and rates change and depend on the seller's taxable status; use the live page and a qualified adviser for filing decisions.

## Official model

- Japanese Consumption Tax obligations depend on the seller's taxable status and registration under the qualified-invoice (invoice/インボイス) system, not on a single fixed rule.
- Sellers can register a qualified-invoice issuer registration number in Seller Central tax settings so that qualifying invoices can be issued; whether registration is required or beneficial depends on the seller's own tax position.
- Consumption tax that Amazon shows on fees or transactions is reporting evidence, not a completed filing on the seller's behalf.

## Required evidence

- legal seller entity, taxable/exempt status, qualified-invoice issuer registration number and status, and filing period;
- transaction/tax and fee report rows showing consumption tax, transaction type, and ship-from/ship-to;
- the current Japan Seller Central JCT/qualified-invoice page or authorized export;
- any National Tax Agency notice or professional-adviser position supplied by the seller.

## Guardrails

- Do not merge JP JCT with US sales tax, UK VAT, or EU VAT into one rule.
- A report is transaction evidence, not a legal conclusion about registration, taxable status, filing, or payment liability.
- Do not assume consumption tax shown by Amazon removes every seller obligation.
- Route any registration, taxable-status, rate, or filing decision to a qualified Japanese tax professional.
