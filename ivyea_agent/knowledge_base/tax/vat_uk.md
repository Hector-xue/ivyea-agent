# Amazon UK VAT diagnostics and liability boundaries

Source basis: Amazon UK's public VAT knowledge resources and the authenticated Seller Central tax settings. VAT rules and rates change and depend on the seller's establishment and registration status; use the live page and a qualified adviser for filing decisions.

## Official model

- UK VAT obligations depend on where the business is established, the goods' location, distance-selling/import facts, and the seller's registration status, not on a single fixed rule.
- Amazon can collect and remit VAT in specific marketplace-facilitator scenarios; where it does, the collected VAT is shown on transaction and tax reports rather than added to the seller's own filing automatically.
- VAT Services on Amazon and the Seller Central VAT/registration-number settings are tools, not a substitute for the seller's legal VAT position.

## Required evidence

- legal seller entity, establishment country, UK VAT registration status/number, and filing period;
- transaction/tax report rows showing marketplace-collected versus seller-liable VAT, ship-from/ship-to, and transaction type;
- the current UK VAT resource page or authorized SP-API/Seller Central tax export;
- any HMRC notice or professional-adviser position supplied by the seller.

## Guardrails

- Do not merge UK VAT with EU VAT, US sales tax, or JP JCT into one rule.
- A report is transaction evidence, not a legal conclusion about registration, establishment, nexus, filing, or payment liability.
- Do not assume Amazon-collected VAT removes every seller obligation.
- Route any registration, threshold, rate, or filing decision to a qualified UK tax professional.
