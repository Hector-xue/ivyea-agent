# Amazon EU VAT diagnostics and liability boundaries

Source basis: Amazon's public EU VAT/tax information pages and the authenticated Seller Central tax settings. VAT rules, rates, and thresholds differ by member state and change over time; use the live page and a qualified adviser for filing decisions.

## Official model

- EU VAT obligations depend on the country of establishment, where stock is held, cross-border/import and One-Stop-Shop facts, and each member state's registration rules — they are not a single EU-wide rate or threshold.
- Amazon can collect and remit VAT in specific marketplace-facilitator or import scenarios; where it does, the collected VAT appears on transaction and tax reports rather than being added to the seller's own filing automatically.
- VAT Services on Amazon and per-country VAT/registration-number settings are tools, not a substitute for the seller's legal VAT position in each member state.

## Required evidence

- legal seller entity, establishment country, per-country EU VAT registration status/numbers, OSS/IOSS status, and filing period;
- transaction/tax report rows showing marketplace-collected versus seller-liable VAT, stock location, ship-from/ship-to, and transaction type;
- the current EU VAT resource page or authorized SP-API/Seller Central tax export;
- any member-state tax-authority notice or professional-adviser position supplied by the seller.

## Guardrails

- Do not apply one member state's rate, threshold, or rule to another, and do not merge EU VAT with UK VAT, US sales tax, or JP JCT.
- A report is transaction evidence, not a legal conclusion about registration, establishment, nexus, filing, or payment liability.
- Do not assume Amazon-collected VAT removes every seller obligation in every member state.
- Route any registration, threshold, rate, OSS/IOSS, or filing decision to a qualified EU tax professional.
