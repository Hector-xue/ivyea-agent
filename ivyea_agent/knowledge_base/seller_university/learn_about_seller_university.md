## What the official source establishes

- Seller University is Amazon’s official seller education resource for learning how to sell on Amazon and use seller tools.
- Training topics can include listing creation, fulfillment, advertising, inventory, brand tools, reporting, and account management.
- Seller University content should be treated as official training guidance but still checked against the current Seller Central page and marketplace rules.
- Training videos and lessons may change as Amazon tools and policies change.

## Operational interpretation for Amazon sellers

- Use Seller University cards as SOP anchors for beginner workflows and IvyeaOps onboarding.
- When a user asks how to operate a Seller Central feature, prefer Seller University/Seller Central official training before community advice.
- Link training workflows to Ivyea diagnostic tools: listing audit, offer audit, patrol, review audit, and account diagnosis.

## Evidence required before action

- User role and experience level, marketplace, seller account eligibility, current Seller Central UI path.
- Official lesson/page date or current availability.
- Whether the task is educational, diagnostic, or a write action requiring approval.

## Guardrails

- Do not present training guidance as a substitute for current Seller Central warnings or policy notices.
- Do not automate write actions based only on training content without account data and approval.
- Do not mix community shortcuts into official SOP cards without labeling them.
