## What the official source establishes

- Amazon seller education explains that a product listing/detail page presents the product to shoppers through title, images, bullet points, description/A+ content, price, variations, and product attributes.
- A strong listing helps shoppers understand what is being sold and supports discoverability and conversion.
- Listing creation requires accurate product information and compliance with Amazon listing policies and category rules.
- Optimization should be based on relevant customer search behavior and truthful product differentiation.

## Operational interpretation for Amazon sellers

- Use ad search term winners as input to listing optimization only when the term accurately describes the product.
- Before scaling ads, ensure the listing communicates the core use case, attributes, trust proof, and objections for the target query cluster.
- For new ASINs, establish a baseline listing before using broad discovery data; otherwise the ad data may reflect listing weakness rather than traffic mismatch.

## Evidence required before action

- Listing draft, product facts, category requirements, keyword/search term data, competitor detail pages, review objections.
- Image quality, price, coupon, rating/review count, inventory, variation setup.
- Policy-sensitive claims and compliance documents.

## Guardrails

- Do not keyword stuff or add terms unrelated to the product.
- Do not use misleading images, claims, or competitor content.
- Do not assume a listing edit is safe without category and policy checks.
