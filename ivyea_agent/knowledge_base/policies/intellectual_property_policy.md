## What the official source establishes

- Amazon intellectual property policy addresses rights such as trademarks, copyrights, patents, and related content or product issues.
- Sellers are responsible for ensuring their products and listing content do not infringe others’ intellectual property rights.
- Brand names, logos, images, compatibility claims, copyrighted content, and product designs can create IP risk.
- Seller Central policy pages and account notifications are authoritative for specific IP complaints.

## Operational interpretation for Amazon sellers

- When optimizing listings from competitor terms, distinguish lawful compatibility language from misleading brand use.
- Do not copy competitor images, bullets, A+ modules, or creative structure as a shortcut for conversion improvement.
- For competitor keyword advertising, keep ad/listing content truthful and avoid implying affiliation where none exists.
- IP complaints can explain listing suppression or sudden ad conversion decline.

## Evidence required before action

- Brand ownership/authorization, trademark search where appropriate, product packaging, compatibility documentation.
- Complaint details, ASIN/listing content, images, backend terms, ads keyword strategy.
- Legal or official Amazon guidance for disputed claims.

## Guardrails

- Do not advise copying competitor content or using protected brand assets without rights.
- Do not treat forum workarounds as safe when IP policy is involved.
- Do not make compatibility claims that cannot be substantiated.
