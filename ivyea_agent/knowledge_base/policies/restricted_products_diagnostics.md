# Restricted products and approval diagnostics

Source basis: Amazon seller-policy overview and authenticated Seller Central Restricted Products help. The current marketplace/category help page and account notice are authoritative.

## Official boundary

Amazon states that some categories and products require approval before listing, and sellers must follow applicable laws, product-safety requirements, and Amazon policies. A product being technically listable in another marketplace or category does not prove eligibility in the affected marketplace.

## Evidence required

- marketplace, ASIN/SKU, product type/category/browse node, brand, condition, fulfillment channel, and intended claims/use;
- exact restriction, suppression, approval, or compliance message and notification date;
- the specific policy/help link shown by Amazon;
- ingredients/materials, labels, packaging, age grading, certifications/test reports, safety data, and regulatory identifiers when relevant;
- approval/application status, case history, and documents Amazon explicitly requested.

## Diagnostic rules

- Separate prohibited products, gated categories/brands, product-safety compliance, dangerous-goods classification, and listing-data errors. They require different evidence and remedies.
- Do not conclude that a product is unrestricted because its title is absent from a generic examples list.
- Do not submit documents until the requested document type, issuer, product scope, dates, and marketplace are confirmed.

## Guardrails

- Do not advise relisting under a false category, changing claims to conceal the actual product, or using another ASIN to bypass enforcement.
- Do not fabricate or alter test reports, invoices, registrations, SDS, or approvals.
- For legal/regulatory questions, identify the evidence gap and recommend qualified professional review where appropriate.
