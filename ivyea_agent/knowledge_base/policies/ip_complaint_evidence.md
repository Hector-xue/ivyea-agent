# Intellectual-property complaint evidence and response boundaries

Source basis: Amazon intellectual-property policy and the account's authenticated complaint/Account Health record. This is operational evidence guidance, not legal advice.

## Classify before responding

Identify the exact complaint type named by Amazon—such as trademark, copyright, patent, counterfeit/authenticity, or another policy issue. Do not treat every IP-related enforcement as the same problem, and do not assume that ownership of inventory grants rights to use another party's images, text, logos, or protected design.

## Evidence intake

- marketplace, complaint ID, rights-owner/contact information as displayed by Amazon, ASIN/SKU, brand, and affected listing content;
- exact notice, policy cited, date, status, and appeal/retraction path;
- invoices and supply-chain records for authenticity questions;
- authorization/license, trademark/brand relationship, original creative files, compatibility evidence, or legal documents relevant to the specific claim;
- every prior submission, rights-owner response, and Amazon response.

## Reasoning contract

- Separate product authenticity, right to resell, and right to use protected listing content/branding.
- Match evidence to the complaint type and exact affected content/product.
- Where the seller disputes legal validity or needs an interpretation of rights, state that qualified legal advice may be required.

## Guardrails

- Never fabricate invoices, authorization letters, licenses, rights-owner communication, or retractions.
- Do not advise contacting a complainant deceptively or pressuring them.
- Do not relist under another ASIN/brand to bypass an unresolved complaint.
- Redact personal contact, bank, tax, and identity data before storing evidence.
