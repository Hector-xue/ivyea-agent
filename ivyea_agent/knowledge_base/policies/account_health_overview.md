## What the official source establishes

- Amazon program policies define seller obligations and customer experience standards.
- Account health issues can arise from order performance, policy violations, product compliance, intellectual property, authenticity, restricted products, or customer experience problems.
- Policy pages and Seller Central notifications are authoritative for required corrective actions.
- Operational tactics must not bypass Amazon policy or manipulate platform trust signals.

## Operational interpretation for Amazon sellers

- When ads or listing diagnostics show suppressed traffic or sudden conversion drops, check account health and listing compliance before optimizing bids.
- Policy-sensitive categories require stricter source validation and may need official documents, approvals, or category-specific guidance.
- Ivyea recommendations should separate growth tactics from compliance remediation.

## Evidence required before action

- Account Health dashboard, performance notifications, listing suppression reason, policy warning, appeal status.
- Product category, claims, compliance documents, IP rights, authenticity evidence.
- Recent listing edits, review events, price changes, and customer complaints.

## Guardrails

- Do not recommend review manipulation, fake orders, policy evasion, or hidden incentivization.
- Do not override Seller Central policy notices with community anecdotes.
- Do not perform irreversible changes without official/account-specific evidence.
