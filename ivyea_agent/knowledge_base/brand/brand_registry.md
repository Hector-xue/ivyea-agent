# Amazon Brand Registry eligibility and evidence

Source basis: Amazon Brand Registry official enrollment pages. Country-specific requirements and accepted trademark offices remain dynamic.

## Official eligibility boundary

- Brand Registry is an Amazon program for protecting and growing a brand; selling in the Amazon store is not itself the eligibility test.
- Enrollment generally requires an eligible active registered trademark or, for supported offices and cases, a pending application.
- The brand name and logo evidence must align with the trademark record and how the mark is permanently affixed to products or packaging.
- The trademark owner should enroll the brand and then add authorized users or agents with appropriate roles.

## Evidence checklist

- target Amazon stores and countries;
- trademark office, registration/application number, status, owner name, mark type, and exact mark text/image;
- product and packaging images showing permanent branding;
- owner, agent, licensee, distributor, and seller-account relationships;
- verification-code correspondence and current Brand Registry role assignments.

## Guardrails

- Do not promise approval based only on having a seller account, domain, logo file, or unregistered brand name.
- Do not assume enrollment in one country automatically resolves every country-specific right or store requirement.
- Brand Registry access is not proof that every catalog contribution, IP complaint, or ownership dispute will be decided in the brand's favor.
