# Amazon Transparency enrollment and serialization boundaries

Source basis: Amazon Transparency official product page. Eligibility, code formats, fees, and operational reviews can change by country and account.

## Official product boundary

- Transparency uses unique serial codes to help verify product authenticity and accuracy.
- A participating product must pass the program's operational review before protected units follow the applicable serialization requirement.
- Codes may be Amazon-issued Transparency codes or eligible serial codes connected through the program.
- Protection applies to both Amazon-fulfilled and seller-fulfilled units under the program's current operating rules.

## Operational evidence

- Brand Registry/Transparency account and user role;
- enrolled product and GTIN/ASIN mapping;
- code source, generation batch, production lot, print/label specification, and activation state;
- unit-level scan and exception records across manufacturing, inbound, fulfillment, returns, and customer verification;
- current program notice for the target marketplace.

## Guardrails

- Do not serialize unenrolled products or reuse, predict, or manufacture codes outside the authorized workflow.
- Enrollment does not replace trademark enforcement, supply-chain controls, or product-safety obligations.
- A failed scan is an investigation signal, not by itself proof of counterfeiting.
