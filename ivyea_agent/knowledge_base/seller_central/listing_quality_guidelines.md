## What the official source establishes

- Seller Central listing quality guidance covers the completeness and quality of product detail page content.
- Important listing assets include title, images, bullet points, description/A+ content where eligible, and product attributes.
- High-quality listings help shoppers understand the product and can affect discoverability and conversion.
- Listing rules and best practices vary by category and marketplace; category-specific style guides may apply.

## Operational interpretation for Amazon sellers

- Before negating a relevant ad term for low conversion, check whether the listing actually answers the shopper intent behind that term.
- Map winning ad search terms back into title, bullets, product attributes, images, and A+ where appropriate and compliant.
- Low CTR often points to main image, price, title, review, or offer competitiveness; low CVR often points to detail-page mismatch or trust issues.
- Listing improvements can lift all traffic sources, so they rank above bid/negative changes in Ivyea optimization priority.

## Evidence required before action

- Listing text, image set, A+ content, price, coupon, rating, review count, Q&A, variation structure.
- Search terms with clicks/orders and terms with high clicks but low conversion.
- Category/style guide requirements and suppressed listing warnings.

## Guardrails

- Do not stuff keywords or make unsupported claims to chase ad terms.
- Do not assume traffic is bad when the listing fails to carry the query intent.
- Do not apply generic title or bullet rules without category-specific checks.
