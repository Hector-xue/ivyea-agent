## What the official source establishes

- Product listing guidelines define how sellers should create and maintain product detail pages.
- Listings must accurately represent the product and follow Amazon requirements for titles, images, descriptions, attributes, and category data.
- Incorrect, incomplete, duplicate, or misleading listings can harm customer experience and may trigger suppression or compliance action.
- Guidelines can vary across product categories and marketplaces.

## Operational interpretation for Amazon sellers

- Treat listing compliance as a precondition for advertising scale; ads cannot reliably fix a weak or suppressed listing.
- Use structured product attributes and backend fields to support discoverability without overloading visible copy.
- For ad feedback loops, update the listing only when the search term is relevant, accurate, and supported by product facts.

## Evidence required before action

- Current listing content, category requirements, suppression warnings, browse node, variation family, product facts.
- Advertising search term data and organic query intent.
- Customer review and return reasons.

## Guardrails

- Do not create duplicate listings to solve ranking or content conflicts.
- Do not misclassify the product to access traffic or bypass restrictions.
- Do not make listing edits that contradict product packaging, compliance documents, or customer expectations.
