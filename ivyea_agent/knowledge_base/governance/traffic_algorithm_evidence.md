# Traffic And Algorithm Claim Evidence Standard

Source type: internal governance
Updated: 2026-07-06

Official reference points:
- https://advertising.amazon.com/library/guides/measure-improve-campaigns
- https://advertising.amazon.com/help/GBYSPTSLR337JMLH
- https://advertising.amazon.com/solutions/products/sponsored-products

## Mandatory classification

Every traffic, auction, attribution, or organic-ranking statement must be labeled as one of:

- Official fact: directly supported by a current Amazon source, with marketplace/product/date scope.
- Account observation: present in an authorized report or console message.
- Account inference: an explanation supported by a reproducible comparison after checking alternatives.
- Operator hypothesis: plausible but not established; requires a test.
- Unknown: evidence is insufficient.

## Claims requiring a block or downgrade

- undocumented traffic pools, fixed weights, hidden quality scores, or rank coefficients;
- guaranteed placement, impression, conversion, or organic-rank outcomes from a bid/budget action;
- treating attributed sales as incremental lift;
- treating a click-filtered search-term report as the complete impression universe;
- applying one ad product's attribution window or sales scope to another product;
- treating community repetition, one account, or one time window as an Amazon-wide rule.

## Required response shape

1. State what the official source actually establishes.
2. State the account observation separately.
3. Mark the proposed explanation as inference or hypothesis.
4. List material alternative explanations and missing evidence.
5. Give a reversible test and a decision threshold when action is appropriate.

Absence of a public formula is not proof that a rumored mechanism is false. It means the answer must remain an evidence-bounded hypothesis.
