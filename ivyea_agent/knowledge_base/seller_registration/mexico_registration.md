# Amazon Mexico seller registration baseline

Source basis: Amazon Mexico's public seller registration guide. Requirements shown in the live registration workflow take precedence over this summary.

## Official baseline

- Registration collects business, primary-contact, billing, store/product, and identity-verification information.
- The public guide asks for government-issued identity evidence and recent proof of residential or business address; the exact accepted document is determined by the live workflow.
- The bank-account holder must match the seller or registered business relationship presented during registration.

## Diagnostic inputs

- business location/type, legal name, tax/registration identifier (e.g. RFC), and registered address;
- primary contact, beneficial-owner/legal-representative role, and bank-account holder;
- exact requested document, rejection text, timestamp, and verification method;
- whether the document is valid, complete, legible, unedited, and consistent with submitted data.

## Guardrails

- Do not reuse a US-only checklist as proof of Mexican acceptance.
- Do not promise a fixed approval time or recommend altering truthful legal data.
- Tax (RFC/consumption-tax) and incorporation questions require current Mexican professional advice.
