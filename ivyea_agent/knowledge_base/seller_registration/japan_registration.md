# Amazon Japan seller registration baseline

Source basis: Amazon Japan's public seller and account-registration guides. This is marketplace-specific guidance for Amazon.co.jp.

## Official preparation baseline

The cited Amazon Japan pages identify preparation items that include an email address, government-issued photo ID, a statement issued within the previous 180 days, telephone number, valid credit card, and a bank account for receiving proceeds. The page notes that a Japanese My Number card is not accepted for the identity-document route described there.

Amazon Japan also describes a post-registration review connected to Japan's Installment Sales Act. The live Seller Central request is authoritative for the information a particular seller must provide and whether additional review remains incomplete.

## Diagnostic intake

- JP marketplace and individual/company registration type;
- exact screen/email and registration stage;
- document type, issuing country, issue date, language, and whether all corners/details are visible;
- submitted legal name/address and the corresponding values on ID, statement, card, and bank records;
- whether Seller Central requests additional information or shows an incomplete Installment Sales Act review;
- case/notification reference and submission timestamps.

## Guardrails

- Do not apply US/UK entity-document assumptions to Japan.
- Do not recommend the My Number card where Amazon's cited guide says it cannot be used for this review.
- Do not infer the rejection reason from delay alone.
- Redact identity numbers, bank details, addresses, phone numbers, and document images before creating a diagnostic record.
