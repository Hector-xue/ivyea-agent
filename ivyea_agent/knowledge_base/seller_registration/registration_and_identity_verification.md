# Seller registration and identity verification baseline

Source basis: Amazon's public seller registration guide for the US registration flow. Requirements can vary by country, legal entity, and the verification route shown in the account.

## Official flow

Amazon's guide groups registration into five stages: business information, seller/primary-contact information, billing information, store and product information, and identity verification.

Key details in the current public guide:

- Enter the business name and registered address as they appear in the relevant government registration record.
- Seller information identifies the business's primary contact and uses government-issued identity information.
- The bank account used for disbursement must be in the primary contact's name or the business's name.
- Identity verification asks for a government-issued ID and proof of residential/business address dated within the last 180 days, such as a bank or credit-card statement.
- The account may ask for a face-and-ID photo or a live/scheduled video call. Follow the route presented in the account.
- Uploaded documents should be high-quality color scans/photos, show all four corners, contain legible text, and be signed where relevant. The guide says not to use screenshots or blurry images.

## Diagnostic intake for a registration problem

Collect the exact on-screen message, registration country/store, business type, which of the five stages failed, document type and issue date, and whether names/addresses differ across registration, ID, proof of address, and bank records. Redact document numbers, images, bank details, and personal identifiers before storing evidence.

## Reasoning boundary

Name/address consistency and image quality are diagnostic checks grounded in the official flow. The cause of a rejection is not proven unless Amazon's message or case response identifies it. Do not promise approval time, invent an undocumented workaround, repeatedly resubmit altered identity data, or advise fabricated/edited documents.

## Escalation

When the public guide does not explain an account-specific rejection, use the authenticated Seller Central help/case path and preserve the case ID and exact response as account-local evidence. Authenticated content must be imported through an authorized export; do not bypass login controls.
